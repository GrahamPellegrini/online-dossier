World Models
as Action Judges

Graham Pellegrini - MSc Artificial Intelligence
WACT: evaluating world model support for action chunk policies
5-minute research pitch - preliminary results surface
01 / 05
Research question

Can a model judge which robot action is more likely to match the expert?

The policy proposes possible wrist motions. The world model scores the likely future produced by each motion before one candidate is selected.

Policy proposes candidate motions
->
World model scores predicted consequences
02 / 05
The comparison

Same action policy.Three ways to select the final motion.

Policy only The actor decides
ACT selects its own top candidate without world-model scoring.
Policy + world model The actor and critic combine
The policy proposes candidates and the world model helps score them.
World model decides The critic chooses
The world model ranks the candidate pool independently of policy preference.
03 / 05
Current evidence
77% lower mean translation error V-JEPA2-AC world-model selection versus policy only.
91.5% head-to-head window win rate Across the current 7,607-window held-out benchmark surface.

These are preliminary thesis-facing numbers from the corrected benchmark surface. Remaining final runs should refine wording, not the purpose of this talk.

04 / 05
Takeaway

World models look useful as offline action judges, but this is not yet deployed success.

The signal is strong World-model guidance improves trajectory fidelity against the policy-only baseline.
The claim is bounded The benchmark measures recorded demonstration fidelity, not physical task completion.
The next test is online The open question is whether better offline trajectories transfer to live control.
